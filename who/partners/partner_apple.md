# Apple

## Overview

Apple is a prospective technology partner for the Context Commons, primarily for **hardware grants** (MacBook Neo workstations for youth builders and community stewards) and integration with Apple's existing **education and community impact programs**. The MacBook Neo is the program's designated personal agentic workstation — the "printing press" each youth builder earns through program completion.

## Organization Profile

- **Organization:** Apple Inc.
- **Type:** Corporate — technology manufacturer
- **Mission alignment:** Apple's education programs emphasize creativity, equity, and accessibility. The Context Commons extends this to agentic literacy — teaching communities to build with AI, not just consume it. Apple's emphasis on privacy aligns with the program's "no data extraction" principle.
- **Geographic presence:** Global; significant presence in both California (LA pilot) and across the US (Grand Rapids pilot)

## Partnership Type & Contributions

- **Partnership type:** Hardware, education, distribution
- **Contribution type:**
  - **MacBook Neo grants** — personal agentic workstations for youth program graduates (earned, not given)
  - **Shared workstations** — community center and classroom deployments
  - **Apple Education programs** — alignment with existing K-12 and community education initiatives
  - **Distribution and visibility** — amplification through Apple's education channels
- **Scale:** Per-cohort hardware needs: 8-16 MacBook Neos per pilot site (youth builders) + 3-8 shared workstations (community centers); ~$30K-$80K per pilot site in hardware value

## Terms & Non-Negotiables

- Community governance is preserved — Apple contributes hardware and programs, not program direction
- No data extraction — community context graphs and usage patterns belong to communities, not Apple
- Open standards — the aDNA ontology and Lattice Protocol remain open; Apple does not acquire exclusive rights
- Hardware is earned — youth builders earn their workstation through program completion, reinforcing dignity through capability

## What We Offer Apple

- **Authentic community impact stories** — real communities using Apple hardware for genuine good, not marketing simulations
- **Model for responsible AI education** — a replicable, governed, community-driven approach Apple can reference and learn from
- **Edge feedback** — community-built systems surface real hardware and software needs from underserved populations
- **Education program innovation** — extends Apple Education beyond content consumption to agentic system creation
