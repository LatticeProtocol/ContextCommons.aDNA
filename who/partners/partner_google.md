# Google

## Overview

Google is a prospective technology partner for the Context Commons, with alignment across **multiple contribution vectors**: the Google.org AI Opportunity Fund (significant funding), Chromebook/device grants for classroom use, Google Cloud compute credits for community-operated agent systems, and API access to Gemini models. Google's stated commitments to AI equity and digital inclusion map directly to the Context Commons mission.

## Organization Profile

- **Organization:** Google LLC / Google.org (philanthropic arm)
- **Type:** Corporate — technology, cloud, AI
- **Mission alignment:** Google.org's AI Opportunity Fund ($75M+ committed) explicitly targets AI skills training for underserved communities. The Context Commons approach — teaching communities to build and govern their own AI systems — extends beyond typical "AI literacy" programs to genuine capability and agency.
- **Geographic presence:** Global; strong LA presence (Google LA offices, YouTube); Michigan presence (Google Ann Arbor)

## Partnership Type & Contributions

- **Partnership type:** Funding, compute, hardware, API access, distribution
- **Contribution type:**
  - **Google.org AI Opportunity Fund** — grant funding ($2M-$30M range for aligned programs)
  - **Google Cloud credits** — compute for community-operated agent systems and development
  - **Chromebook grants** — supplementary classroom devices via Google for Education / Google Nonprofits
  - **API access** — Gemini API access for curriculum development and community agent systems
  - **Google for Nonprofits** — suite of tools (Workspace, Ad Grants, YouTube Nonprofit Program)
- **Scale:** AI Opportunity Fund grants range from $2M-$30M; Chromebook programs provide per-classroom device sets; Cloud credits typically $10K-$100K for nonprofits

## Terms & Non-Negotiables

- Community governance is preserved — Google contributes resources, not program direction
- No data extraction — community context graphs and usage patterns belong to communities; Google does not acquire rights to community-generated training data
- Open standards — the aDNA ontology and Lattice Protocol remain open; Context Commons is not a Google-exclusive program
- Multi-model approach — the program teaches agentic systems design using open standards; it is not tied to a single vendor's AI models

## What We Offer Google

- **AI equity impact at scale** — a structured, governed, replicable model for AI skills in underserved communities, aligned with Google.org's stated priorities
- **Authentic adoption stories** — real communities directing AI systems for real needs, not demonstration projects
- **Responsible AI model** — community governance + open standards + no extraction = a model Google can point to
- **Education program differentiation** — goes beyond "learn to prompt" to "learn to build, govern, and operate context-driven AI systems"
