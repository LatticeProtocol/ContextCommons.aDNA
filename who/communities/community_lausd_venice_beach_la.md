# LAUSD / Venice Beach Book Club / UCLA — Los Angeles, CA

> **DRAFT** — This profile has not been reviewed by community representatives. Per Standing Order #7, all community content requires community review before it is considered accurate. `#needs-community-review`

## Overview

The Los Angeles pilot site (**Pilot B**) is a multi-institutional constellation: **LAUSD** (Los Angeles Unified School District) for youth access and school integration, the **Venice Beach Book Club** as a community-rooted intergenerational space, and **UCLA** as an academic research and mentorship partner. This distributed model tests the Context Commons approach in a large, diverse urban environment — contrasting with the concentrated church-based model in Grand Rapids.

## Location & Demographics

- **Location:** Los Angeles, California, USA — spanning Westside (Venice, Santa Monica) to broader LAUSD service area
- **Population:** ~4M city / ~13M metro; LAUSD serves ~420,000 students across 1,000+ schools
- **Languages:** English, Spanish (40%+ of LAUSD students), Korean, Armenian, Tagalog, Mandarin, Vietnamese, and 100+ additional languages
- **Key demographics:** Extraordinarily diverse student population; significant youth populations underserved by existing tech ed; large elder communities navigating complex healthcare and immigration systems; strong neighborhood identity (Venice, Mar Vista, Culver City, South LA)

## Infrastructure

- **Internet:** Generally good broadband access; digital divide persists in lower-income neighborhoods; school Wi-Fi available during school hours
- **Physical spaces:** LAUSD school facilities (computer labs, classrooms), Venice Beach Book Club meeting space (community-oriented, informal), UCLA campus facilities for mentor training and research
- **Devices:** LAUSD provides Chromebooks to students (1:1 in many schools); personal device ownership varies; UCLA has lab facilities; Venice Beach Book Club has limited tech infrastructure
- **Hardware needs:** MacBook Neo workstations for youth builders (beyond school Chromebooks), shared equipment for Venice Beach Book Club sessions, portable demo kits for community events

## Community Needs

- **Educational equity:** LAUSD's 420K students include large populations underserved by traditional CS education; Context Commons offers an alternative to standard "learn to code" that emphasizes community agency
- **Immigration and civic navigation:** Large immigrant communities need help with legal paperwork, benefits enrollment, school system navigation, civic participation — tasks that agentic systems can support when governed by community context
- **Elder care and health navigation:** Venice and surrounding neighborhoods have aging populations navigating Medicare, housing support, and healthcare coordination
- **Cross-generational connection:** In a sprawling city, intergenerational knowledge transfer is fractured; the program rebuilds this loop intentionally
