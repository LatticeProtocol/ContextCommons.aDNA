# Peter's Church — Grand Rapids, MI

> **DRAFT** — This profile has not been reviewed by community representatives. Per Standing Order #7, all community content requires community review before it is considered accurate. `#needs-community-review`

## Overview

Peter's Church is the anchor site for **Pilot A** of the Context Commons program. Located in Grand Rapids, Michigan, the church serves as an intergenerational hub with existing infrastructure for youth programming, elder care coordination, and community organizing. Grand Rapids offers a compelling combination: a mid-sized city with strong faith-based community networks, a growing technology sector, and underserved populations who stand to benefit most from agentic literacy.

## Location & Demographics

- **Location:** Grand Rapids, Kent County, Michigan, USA
- **Population:** ~200,000 city / ~1.1M metro area
- **Languages:** English (primary), Spanish, Swahili, Karen, Arabic (significant immigrant/refugee communities)
- **Key demographics:** Diverse intergenerational congregation; significant youth population (14-24) underserved by existing tech education; elder members navigating complex healthcare and benefits systems; growing immigrant and refugee community with multilingual needs

## Infrastructure

- **Internet:** Municipal broadband available in downtown corridor; reliability varies by neighborhood; some homes limited to mobile data
- **Physical spaces:** Church hall (capacity TBD), classrooms suitable for small-group sessions, kitchen/community gathering space for intergenerational events
- **Devices:** Limited personal device ownership among target youth; shared computers at church and public library; mobile phones prevalent but not suitable for development work
- **Hardware needs:** MacBook Neo workstations for youth builders (earned through program completion), shared workstations for community center, display/projection for group sessions

## Community Needs

- **Elder care navigation:** Complex healthcare system, Medicare/Medicaid paperwork, appointment scheduling, medication management — administrative burden on elderly congregation members and their families
- **Immigrant services:** Immigration paperwork, language access, benefits enrollment, civic participation support for growing refugee and immigrant community
- **Youth opportunity gap:** Young people in the community lack access to high-quality technology education and career pathways; existing programs focus on consumption, not creation
- **Community coordination:** Church and neighborhood organizations coordinate informally; no structured system for matching needs with resources across generations
