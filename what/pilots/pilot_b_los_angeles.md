# Pilot B — LAUSD / Venice Beach Book Club / UCLA, Los Angeles, CA

> **DRAFT** — This pilot plan has not been reviewed by community representatives. Per Standing Order #7, all community content requires community review before it is considered accurate. `#needs-community-review`

## Overview

Pilot B is the **distributed, multi-institutional model** for the Context Commons program. Where Grand Rapids tests depth in a concentrated, church-based setting, Los Angeles tests breadth across a constellation of three institutions: **LAUSD** (youth access through schools), the **Venice Beach Book Club** (intergenerational community anchor), and **UCLA** (academic research and mentorship partner). This model tests whether the circular knowledge economy can function when governance is distributed across institutions with different missions, calendars, and administrative structures.

Pilot B launches in Q4 2026, one quarter after Pilot A. This sequencing is deliberate: Pilot A learnings inform Pilot B design, curriculum localization, and logistics planning. However, Pilot B is not a copy of Pilot A — it faces fundamentally different challenges (multi-site coordination, extraordinary linguistic diversity, institutional politics) and must develop its own solutions (Standing Order #9).

## Governance: Distributed Model

Unlike Grand Rapids (single institution, single governance body), Los Angeles requires distributed governance across three institutions with different decision-making cultures:

| Institution | Governance Culture | Pilot Role | Steward Type |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------|-------------|
| **LAUSD** | Bureaucratic — school board, union, administrative hierarchy | Youth access, school-based sessions, student recruitment | Teacher champion — someone who can navigate LAUSD internally |
| **Venice Beach Book Club** | Informal — community-driven, consensus-oriented | Intergenerational anchor, elder engagement, community identity | Community organizer — trusted by both youth and elders |
| **UCLA** | Academic — faculty governance, IRB, research protocols | Research partner, student mentors, evaluation, grant co-PI | Faculty advisor — community-engaged research expertise |

**Coordination mechanism**: Monthly steward council (all stewards from all 3 institutions) to coordinate scheduling, resolve cross-institutional issues, and make collective governance decisions. No single institution has veto over another institution's domain. Cross-domain decisions (e.g., data sharing between sites) require consensus of all stewards. Per Standing Order #11, community governance is final — institutional administration cannot override steward decisions about the pilot.

**Multi-institutional MOU**: Before pilot launch, all three institutions sign a Memorandum of Understanding specifying: participation scope, resource commitments, data governance terms, scheduling coordination responsibilities, and the principle that community governance (steward council) prevails over institutional preferences when they conflict.
