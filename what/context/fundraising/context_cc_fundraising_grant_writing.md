# Grant Writing for Context Commons

## The Challenge

CC sits at the intersection of multiple funder categories — AI education, workforce development, digital equity, elder care, community development, youth empowerment. This is a strength (many doors to knock on) and a risk (sounding like everything to everyone). Every application must frame CC through the specific lens the funder cares about, while staying honest to the program's actual design.

## Positioning by Funder Priority

| Funder Priority | CC Framing | Lead With |
|----------------|------------|-----------|
| STEM / AI Education | Youth builders learning to build and direct agentic AI systems | Curriculum tracks, capstone projects, career pathways |
| Workforce Development | Youth 14-24 gaining career-grade technical skills | Builder track competencies, mentor network, hardware earned |
| Digital Equity | Communities gaining ownership of AI tools, not just access | Community governance model, design-for-margins, bilingual support |
| Elder Care / Aging | AI-assisted navigation of healthcare, benefits, housing | Elder track use cases, intergenerational loop, dignity-centered design |
| AI Safety / Responsible AI | Community-governed AI with human oversight and local values | Context graphs as alignment mechanism, steward governance, open standards |
| Community Development | Intergenerational mentorship rebuilding local knowledge infrastructure | Circular knowledge economy, faith-based partnerships, mutual benefit |
| Racial Equity | Underserved youth gaining technological agency and economic opportunity | Demographics of pilot communities, design-for-margins principle |

## The Printing Press Narrative

CC's strongest rhetorical asset is the Gutenberg metaphor: "Every community deserves its own printing press. We are here to help them build it." Use this narrative to:
- Open LOIs and executive summaries — it makes the abstract concrete
- Explain why community ownership matters (the press serves the people, not the press manufacturer)
- Distinguish CC from "AI literacy" programs that teach consumption, not creation

Reference `context_cc_thesis.md` for the full thesis. Do not repeat it in applications — distill it to 2-3 sentences that match the funder's language.

## Letter of Inquiry (LOI) Components

Most foundation grants start with a 2-3 page LOI. Include:

1. **Problem statement** (1 paragraph) — Frame using the funder's priority from the table above
2. **Proposed approach** (2-3 paragraphs) — CC's three-tier mentorship model, curriculum tracks, community governance
3. **Target population** (1 paragraph) — Specific pilot community demographics, youth ages 14-24, underserved indicators
4. **Expected outcomes** (bulleted) — Quantifiable: participants trained, systems built, elders served, hardware deployed
5. **Budget range** (1 line) — Match to funder's typical grant size
6. **Organizational capacity** (1 paragraph) — Lattice Protocol infrastructure, aDNA standard, fiscal sponsor (if applicable)

## Full Application Sections

Federal and major foundation applications typically require:

- **Need statement**: Data-backed description of the gap CC addresses. Use local data for pilot communities (Grand Rapids poverty/education stats, LAUSD English learner demographics).
- **Program design**: Curriculum Council, four tracks, three-tier mentorship, timeline. Include logic model diagram.
- **Evaluation plan**: Pre/post AI literacy assessment (design this before applying), attendance tracking, outcome metrics, qualitative stories.
- **Organizational capacity**: Team bios, fiscal sponsor's track record, partnership letters.
- **Budget and budget narrative**: Line items with justification. Hardware, stipends, API costs, travel, fiscal sponsor fees.
- **Sustainability plan**: How CC continues after the grant period — diversification model, earned revenue, other funders.

## Common Pitfalls

- **Jargon overload**: "Agentic DNA Context Graph Standard" means nothing to a program officer. Say "a system for organizing community knowledge so AI tools can use it faithfully."
- **Technology-first framing**: Funders fund outcomes for people, not software architecture. Lead with community benefit.
- **Vague outcomes**: "Increase AI literacy" is unmeasurable. "20 youth builders complete capstone projects deploying community-serving AI systems" is specific.
- **Missing the governance story**: CC's community governance model is a differentiator — most AI education programs don't have it. Always include it.
