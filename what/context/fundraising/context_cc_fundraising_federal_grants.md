# Federal Grant Landscape

> **Deadline volatility**: Federal grant cycles are annual but dates shift. Verify all deadlines at grants.gov or the agency website before starting any application. Last verified: April 2026.

## Why Federal Grants Matter for CC

Federal grants provide the largest individual awards ($300K-$2M+) and carry institutional legitimacy that unlocks follow-on funding. They also require the most complex applications and strictest compliance. CC should pursue 2-3 federal applications per year, starting with the highest-alignment programs.

## Priority Federal Programs

### Tier 1 — Highest Alignment

| Program | Agency | Range | Cycle | CC Alignment |
|---------|--------|-------|-------|-------------|
| **CSforAll: Research & RPPs** | NSF | $300K-$2M | Annual (Feb) | Core fit — Research-Practice Partnership model matches CC's community-researcher structure. Requires university partner (GVSU for Grand Rapids, UCLA for LA). |
| **YouthBuild** | DOL | $1-2M (~57 awards) | Annual (Mar) | Strong — ages 16-24, now requires AI literacy component, faith-based orgs eligible. Ideal for Grand Rapids pilot. |
| **National Leadership Grants for Libraries** | IMLS | $25K-$1M (~45 awards) | Annual (Mar) | Strong — explicitly prioritizes AI literacy, community engagement, digital inclusion. Partner with local libraries as community hubs. |
| **AmeriCorps State & National** | AmeriCorps | $100K-$1M+ | Annual (Mar) | Strong — FY2026 prioritizes faith-based orgs and concentrated poverty. Fund CC mentors/facilitators as AmeriCorps members. |

### Tier 2 — Strong Alignment, Watch for Cycles

| Program | Agency | Range | Cycle | CC Alignment |
|---------|--------|-------|-------|-------------|
| **CIVIC Innovation Challenge** | NSF | Stage 1: $75K; Stage 2: $1M | Watch for CIVIC 4.0 | Elder care and healthcare navigation use cases are textbook CIVIC projects. Two-stage model reduces initial application burden. |
| **ReDDDoT** | NSF | Phase 1: $75K; Phase 2: $1.5M | Watch FY26 | Responsible AI with community co-design. Ford Foundation co-funding adds legitimacy. |
| **ITEST** | NSF | $25-30M pool annually | Annual | PreK-12 youth STEM engagement, requires equity + career prep components. |
| **Innovative Approaches to Literacy** | ED | Competition-based | FY2026 TBD | High-need LEAs; LAUSD pilot could leverage for bilingual/digital literacy. |

## Application Strategy

1. **Start with IMLS or AmeriCorps** — simpler applications than NSF, strong CC alignment, builds track record
2. **Pursue NSF CSforAll RPP as the flagship** — largest potential award, but requires university partnership and 6-8 week application effort
3. **Layer state grants** (MiSTEM, ETPP) as supplemental funding for pilot-specific costs
4. **Watch for CIVIC 4.0 and ReDDDoT** — two-stage models mean lower initial investment to apply

## Key Requirement: University Partnership

NSF programs (CSforAll RPP, CIVIC, ReDDDoT) strongly prefer or require institutional partners:
- **Grand Rapids**: Grand Valley State University (GVSU) — education and computing departments
- **Los Angeles**: UCLA — education, computer science, or public policy departments
- Begin cultivating these relationships before application season
