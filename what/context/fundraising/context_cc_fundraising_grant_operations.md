# Grant Operations

## The Operational Reality

Winning a grant is the beginning of the work, not the end. CC must build compliance infrastructure, tracking systems, and reporting capacity before — not after — receiving its first award. The most common failure mode for new programs is not failing to win grants but failing to manage them: co-mingled funds, missing time documentation, and late reports that poison funder relationships.

## Seven-Stage Grant Pipeline

Every grant opportunity flows through seven stages. The operational pipeline at `how/pipelines/grant_pipeline/` tracks individual opportunities through these stages.

| Stage | Duration | Activities | Output |
|-------|----------|-----------|--------|
| **1. Prospect** | Ongoing | Research funders, monitor deadlines, add to tracking system | Prospect entry with alignment notes |
| **2. Qualify** | 2-4 weeks | Assess fit (priorities, geography, grant size, eligibility), attend webinars, request pre-application meetings | Go/no-go decision |
| **3. LOI** | 1-2 weeks | Draft 2-3 page Letter of Inquiry (30-50% advance to full proposals) | Submitted LOI |
| **4. Full Application** | 3-6 weeks (6-8 for federal) | Narrative, budget, budget narrative, letters of support, logic model | Submitted application |
| **5. Negotiate** | 2-4 weeks | Sign grant agreement, establish budget tracking codes, set reporting calendar | Executed agreement |
| **6. Implement** | 12 months typical | Deliver program, collect data, submit interim/final reports | Reports filed, outcomes documented |
| **7. Closeout** | Final 2 months | Final reports, reconcile expenditures, document outcomes, begin renewal conversation | Grant closed, renewal LOI if applicable |

## Pre-Application Checklist

Complete before submitting the first grant application:

- [ ] Organization/program description at 50, 100, 250, and 500-word lengths
- [ ] Mission statement (1 sentence)
- [ ] Logic model / Theory of Change diagram
- [ ] Program budget (12-month with line items)
- [ ] Leader bios and resumes (2-3 key people)
- [ ] Board/advisory list with affiliations
- [ ] Letters of support from partner organizations (schools, libraries, community orgs)
- [ ] Fiscal sponsor agreement (or 501(c)(3) determination letter)
- [ ] Financial statements (via fiscal sponsor)
- [ ] Data collection plan (what you measure and how)
- [ ] Pre/post AI literacy assessment instrument (10-question minimum)
- [ ] Participant tracking system (even Google Form → Sheet with demographics, attendance, pre/post scores)
- [ ] Photo/media release forms
- [ ] Time tracking system for anyone paid by grant funds

## Tracking System

### Phase 1 — Spreadsheet (start here)

Essential columns: Funder name, Grant program, Pipeline stage, Deadline dates (LOI/application/reports), Amount requested/awarded, Contact person, Internal owner, Notes/next action, Link to documents.

### Phase 2 — Lightweight CRM (when managing 10+ active grants)

Options: GrantHub by Foundant (purpose-built for small nonprofits), Monday.com Grants Management template, Instrumentl (discovery + tracking).

## Compliance Framework

### Foundation Reporting (Simpler)

- Interim and final narrative reports (what you did, who you served, what changed)
- Financial reports against approved budget categories
- Participant stories and quotes (qualitative impact)
- Frequency: once or twice during a 12-month grant

### Federal Reporting (Complex)

- Federal Financial Reports (SF-425) on quarterly or semi-annual schedules
- Performance progress reports aligned to approved logic models
- Time-and-effort documentation for all grant-funded staff (highest-frequency audit finding)
- Compliance with 2 CFR Part 200 (Uniform Guidance): allowable costs, procurement, subrecipient monitoring
- Single Audit required if expending $750K+ federal annually (threshold increasing to $1M for FY ending after 9/30/2025)
- Every reported figure must trace back to source data through audit trail

## Year 1 Operational Playbook

Three roles needed (can be part-time):
- **Grant Lead** (15-20 hrs/week): prospect research, writing, submissions, funder relationships
- **Program Lead** (20-30 hrs/week): program delivery, data collection, partner relationships
- **Finance/Admin** (5-10 hrs/week): budget tracking, compliance, reporting

| Months | Focus |
|--------|-------|
| 1-2 | Build prospect list (30-50 funders). Set up tracking. Write boilerplate. Research fiscal sponsors. Launch donation page. |
| 3-4 | Submit 3-4 LOIs to local foundations. Apply to 1-2 tech company programs. Begin pilot programming. Collect baseline data. |
| 5-6 | Follow up on LOIs. Write 2-3 full proposals. Secure first corporate sponsor. Document early stories/outcomes. |
| 7-8 | Continue pipeline. Submit 2-3 more applications. Research federal/state grants for year 2. Launch monthly giving. |
| 9-10 | Expect first award decisions. Set up compliance. Submit first grant reports if awarded early. |
| 11-12 | Year-end push: submit before calendar deadlines. Annual report. Compile outcome data for year 2 proposals. |
