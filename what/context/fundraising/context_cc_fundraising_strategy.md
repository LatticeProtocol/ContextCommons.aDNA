# Fundraising Strategy

## Why This Matters

Every community printing press needs ink and paper. CC's mission — putting agentic AI tools into community hands — requires hardware (MacBooks for youth graduates, workstations for stewards), API access, physical space, mentor stipends, and pilot logistics. Grant funding is how CC pays for these without extracting value from the communities it serves.

The funding strategy must reflect CC's values: community governance is non-negotiable, no funder acquires direction over curriculum or community priorities, and open standards remain open.

## Five-Tier Diversification Model

A healthy funding model avoids dependence on any single source:

| Tier | Source | Target % | Strategy |
|------|--------|----------|----------|
| 1 | Foundation Grants | 40-50% | 8-12 applications/year to land 3-5 awards |
| 2 | Corporate Sponsorships | 15-25% | Frame as community investment; offer impact stories, not governance |
| 3 | Individual Donors | 10-20% | Start with Lattice Protocol community; monthly recurring donors |
| 4 | Earned Revenue | 5-15% (growing) | Fee-for-service workshops, curriculum licensing, consulting |
| 5 | Government Funding | Longer-term | Workforce development, digital equity programs (pursue in year 2-3) |

**Additional channel**: Donor-Advised Funds (DAFs) — tech professionals hold DAFs through Fidelity Charitable, Schwab, Silicon Valley CF. Make it easy to recommend grants to CC (or its fiscal sponsor).

## Portfolio Stacking Strategy

CC's program touches multiple funder priorities simultaneously. A realistic Year 1-2 portfolio stacks complementary grants:

| Program | Funder Type | Range | CC Component Funded |
|---------|-------------|-------|---------------------|
| NSF CSforAll RPP | Federal | $300K-$2M | Curriculum R&D + evaluation (requires university partner) |
| DOL YouthBuild | Federal | $1-2M | Grand Rapids pilot operations (youth 16-24, faith-based eligible) |
| IMLS National Leadership | Federal | $25K-$1M | Library hub infrastructure for community access |
| AmeriCorps | Federal | $100K-$1M+ | Mentor/facilitator staffing (cost-per-member model) |
| CA ETPP | State | Up to $16.3M pool | LA pilot operations (English learners, opportunity youth) |
| MiSTEM Mini Grants | State | Up to $50K | Grand Rapids supplemental STEM funding |
| Foundation grants | Private | $50K-$500K | Unrestricted or program-specific (see foundation catalog) |

**Total potential portfolio**: ~$3.5-4M across 2 years. This is ceiling, not floor — realistic Year 1 target is $75K-$150K while building track record.

## Year 1 Targets

- Submit 10-15 grant applications
- Secure 3-5 awards
- Build donor base of 50-100 individuals
- Land 1-2 corporate sponsors
- Total first-year fundraising: $75K-$150K

## Framing Principles

1. **Frame the problem as digital equity, not technology.** Not "understanding transformer architectures" but "communities being left behind by technological change."
2. **Mirror funder language.** Research what each funder has funded before. Use their terminology and priorities.
3. **Show collaboration over solo heroism.** Formalize partnerships with schools, libraries, community centers. Letters of support from superintendents and library directors.
4. **Start with small, local funders.** Community foundations have simpler applications, faster decisions, and are more forgiving of new grantees. Build track record before pursuing national foundations.
5. **Present a clear logic model.** Inputs → Activities → Outputs → Outcomes. Reviewers expect evidence of learning processes, not just enrollment numbers.

## Pre-501(c)(3) Reality

CC may not have independent tax-exempt status initially. This shapes strategy:
- Many grants require 501(c)(3) status — use a **fiscal sponsor** to access these (see fiscal sponsorship context file)
- Some funders accept fiscally sponsored projects directly; others require the fiscal sponsor to be the applicant
- Budget 5-10% of grant revenue for fiscal sponsorship fees
- Plan the 501(c)(3) transition for when annual revenue justifies independent status (typically $250K+/year)
