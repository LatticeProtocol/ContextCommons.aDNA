# Curriculum Framework: Track Structures

Four curriculum tracks define who learns what, when, and in what sequence. All tracks are vertically integrated through the aDNA standard — the curriculum is itself a context graph (Standing Order #8).

## Track Overview

| Track | Duration | Hours | Format | Prerequisite | Primary Audience |
|-------|----------|-------|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Core** | 4 weeks | ~20h | 2×/week, 2.5h sessions | None | All participants |
| **Builder** | 12 weeks | ~80h | 2×/week, 3h sessions + capstone | Core Track | Youth (ages 14-24) |
| **Steward** | 8 weeks | ~40h | Weekly, 2.5h sessions + practicum | Core Track | Community leaders |
| **Elder & Community** | 6 weeks | ~15h | Weekly, 2.5h sessions | None (Core recommended) | Elders, families, community members |

## Core Track (4 weeks, 8 sessions)

**Purpose**: Build shared vocabulary and foundational understanding across all roles.

| Week | Sessions | Topics |
|------|----------|--------|
| 1 | 1-2 | What is a context graph? What is an agent? Using AI vs. directing AI |
| 2 | 3-4 | The aDNA ontology: WHO / WHAT / HOW triad. Identifying triad elements in your community |
| 3 | 5-6 | Privacy, consent, data sovereignty: your context is yours. Community data ownership |
| 4 | 7-8 | Hands-on: build your first personal context graph. Reflection and track selection |

**Completion criteria**: Can articulate the WHO/WHAT/HOW triad, build a basic personal context graph, and explain why community data sovereignty matters. Assessment is formative — no formal gate.

## Builder Track (12 weeks, 24 sessions)

**Purpose**: Develop capable agent-system builders who deploy working tools for their community.

| Phase | Weeks | Sessions | Focus |
|-------|-------|----------|-------|
| **Foundation** | 1-4 | 1-8 | Workstation setup, Claude Code basics, first guided agent deployment |
| **Craft** | 5-8 | 9-16 | Context engineering, prompt engineering, independent context graphs for real community needs |
| **Capstone** | 9-12 | 17-24 | Capstone scoping with steward (wk 9-10), build (wk 10-11), evaluation (wk 12) |

**Progression gates**: B1 at week 4 (workstation operational, first guided agent), B2 at week 8 (independent context graph). Gates are assessed by Lattice mentor with steward input. See [[context_cc_competency_milestones]] for rubrics.

**Capstone**: A working agent system addressing a community-identified need. Evaluated by three reviewers (mentor, steward, end user) against four criteria. See [[context_cc_assessment_framework]] for evaluation details.

**Workstation earning**: Builders who pass all gates and capstone earn a MacBook Neo agentic workstation — Core + Builder completion, 80% attendance, steward endorsement, capstone acceptance.

## Steward Track (8 weeks, 8 sessions)

**Purpose**: Equip community leaders to govern, audit, and sustain local agent systems.

| Phase | Weeks | Sessions | Focus |
|-------|-------|----------|-------|
| **Governance** | 1-4 | 1-4 | Governance fundamentals, needs assessment methods, community audit techniques |
| **Practice** | 5-8 | 5-8 | Co-facilitate a Builder session, practicum with Lattice mentor, sustainability planning |

**Progression gates**: S1 at week 4 (needs assessment completed, governance principles articulated), S2 at week 6 (co-facilitation completed). See [[context_cc_competency_milestones]].

**Practicum**: Stewards shadow a Lattice mentor and co-facilitate at least one Builder Track session. The practicum develops the judgment needed to oversee community agent systems — not to become developers, but to become informed governors (Vygotsky, 1978 — scaffolded within zone of proximal development).

## Elder & Community Member Track (6 weeks, 6 sessions)

**Purpose**: Enable elders and community members to use, co-design, and validate agent systems built for them.

| Week | Session | Focus |
|------|---------|-------|
| 1 | 1 | What agents can do for you — practical demonstrations in participant's language and domain |
| 2 | 2 | Communicating needs: how to describe problems so builders can build solutions |
| 3 | 3 | Co-design workshop 1: elders define problems, youth prototype |
| 4 | 4 | Co-design workshop 2: review prototypes, provide feedback |
| 5 | 5 | Ongoing access: using the systems built for you, requesting changes |
| 6 | 6 | Reflection: what worked, what didn't, what comes next |

**Assessment**: Participation-based with dignity. No formal testing. Self-assessment plus steward confirmation. See [[context_cc_competency_milestones]] for milestone definitions.

## Inter-Track Relationships

The four tracks form the circular knowledge economy described in the program thesis:

```
         Elders (Context & Use Cases)
              ↕          ↕
    Stewards ←→ Youth Builders
    (Governance)  (Capability)
              ↕
        Lattice Mentors
        (Technical Support)
```

- Elders provide problems and domain knowledge → Builders create solutions → Stewards govern the process
- Builders teach elders to use systems → Elders validate community value → Stewards ensure governance compliance
- Mentors support stewards (technical foundations) → Stewards support builders (community context) → All three evaluate capstones

Each track is incomplete without the others. This is by design (Lave & Wenger, 1991 — learning is participation in a community of practice, not individual skill acquisition).

## Localization

Track structures are the master framework. Pilot communities fork and adapt:

| Dimension | Grand Rapids (Pilot A) | Los Angeles (Pilot B) |
|-----------|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Scheduling** | Evening sessions at Peter's Church; weekends for youth with school | Distributed across LAUSD, Venice Beach Book Club, UCLA; multiple time slots |
| **Language** | Primarily English | English, Spanish minimum; 100+ community languages accommodated |
| **Format** | Single-site, concentrated cohort | Multi-site, distributed cohort with coordination sessions |
| **Governance** | Church leadership as steward base (homogeneous) | Multi-institutional steward network (heterogeneous) |

Localization patches are controlled by communities. The Curriculum Council reviews adaptations quarterly and approves structural changes to the master framework (Standing Order #9).
