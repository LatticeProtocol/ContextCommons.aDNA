# Core Principles

## People as Partners, Not Products

The Lattice Protocol exists because we believe the fundamental unit of AI value is **context** — and context belongs to the people and communities who generate it. The aDNA standard is designed so that communities own, govern, and benefit from their own structured knowledge. No extraction. No platform lock-in. No surveillance-as-a-service.

Every system built through the Context Commons is:

- **Locally governed** by community stewards, not by Lattice or any technology partner.
- **Interoperable** via the aDNA standard, so communities can collaborate with each other without surrendering autonomy.
- **Transparent** in what the agents know, how they act, and who they serve.

## The Agentic Printing Press

When Gutenberg's press arrived, it did not matter because books existed. It mattered because communities — churches, universities, guilds — could produce and share their own knowledge on their own terms. The agentic systems now emerging are the next printing press. The Context Commons exists to ensure that this press belongs to communities, not only to the companies that built the type.

Each young person who completes the program earns a **MacBook Neo** — configured as their personal agentic workstation, their printing press — equipped with the tools, context graphs, and training to build and operate context-driven agent systems for their family, their elders, their community, and eventually their careers.

## Operational Principles

1. **Community governance is non-negotiable.** Technology partners contribute resources, not direction. Curriculum Council and community stewards set priorities.
2. **No data extraction.** Community context graphs belong to communities. No partner acquires rights to community-generated context, training data, or usage patterns.
3. **Open standards.** The aDNA ontology and Lattice Protocol remain open. No partner acquires exclusive rights to the infrastructure.
4. **Design for the margins.** If it works for a community without reliable broadband, it works everywhere. Never assume resources.
5. **Bidirectional learning.** Every relationship in the program flows both ways. Mentors learn from communities. Youth learn from elders. Stewards learn from builders.
6. **Real problems, real systems.** Every capstone project addresses a community-identified need. No toy problems. No simulations. Real agents serving real people.
7. **Dignity through capability.** The program builds agency, not dependency. Participants leave with skills, hardware, and the ability to operate independently.
